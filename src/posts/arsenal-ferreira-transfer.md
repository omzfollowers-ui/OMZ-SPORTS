Arsenal are closing in on the signing of Sporting CP midfielder Tomás Ferreira, with sources on both sides describing a deal worth an initial €62m as "advanced." Personal terms — a five-year contract with performance triggers tied to appearances and Champions League progress — were settled earlier in the week.

The 21-year-old has been the standout name on the club's shortlist since March, valued for his range of passing and an unusually mature sense of when to break lines and when to hold. Recruitment staff have tracked him across two seasons, and the player is understood to favour the move despite late interest from two clubs in Germany.

Sporting, who triggered the conversation by signing a replacement last month, are holding firm on add-ons that could push the total fee beyond €70m. That gap is now the only meaningful obstacle, and neither club expects it to derail the transfer.

A medical is being lined up for early next week. Barring a complication, the announcement should land before the international window closes — comfortably inside the timeline Arsenal set themselves when the window opened.
