Pep Guardiola does not do self-pity. He does not do rebuilds either — at least not in the conventional sense. What he does, with an almost unsettling regularity, is reassess, reorganise, and return stronger. And everything emerging from the Etihad Campus this summer suggests that Manchester City's 2026-27 Champions League campaign is being assembled with one singular purpose: redemption.

City's 2025-26 season ended without a Premier League title for the first time in four years, with Arsenal pipping them to the crown on the final day at the Etihad in circumstances that Guardiola described in his post-match press conference as "unacceptable." The Champions League exit at the quarter-final stage against Bayern Munich added further fuel to a narrative the club were desperate to extinguish.

The response has been swift and emphatic. City have already secured the signing of Sporting CP midfielder Mateus Fernandes for £55 million, bringing in the 22-year-old as a long-term successor to the aging Bernardo Silva. Three further targets are understood to be in advanced negotiations, including a centre-back from Serie A and a striker capable of leading the line should Erling Haaland's injury concerns persist.

"We know what we need to do. We know what we have not done well enough," said Guardiola in his end-of-season review. "Next year we will be ready."

The Champions League group stage draw takes place in late August. Europe's elite clubs would be wise to heed the warning.
