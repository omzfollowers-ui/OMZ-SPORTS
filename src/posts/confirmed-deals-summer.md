The window has been open eleven days and already the running total of confirmed business has cleared half a billion euros. Below is where things stand on the moves that are no longer rumours but signatures on contracts.

The headline arrival remains the €54m centre-forward who finally got his move to the Premier League after two summers of speculation. Medical passed, shirt number assigned, unveiling done in front of a sold-out club shop. His former side reinvested almost immediately, bringing in a younger replacement from Brazil for a fee that could rise with appearances.

In Italy, a free transfer has quietly become one of the smartest pieces of business of the window — a 29-year-old defender with 300 top-flight appearances arriving on a two-year deal that costs nothing but wages. Clubs operating under tighter financial rules are increasingly building around exactly these signings.

Elsewhere, two goalkeepers have swapped leagues, a long-serving captain has departed for a final payday abroad, and a teenager few outside the academy had heard of in January has just become his country's most expensive export. The window rarely slows from here. We will keep this list current as the deals land.
