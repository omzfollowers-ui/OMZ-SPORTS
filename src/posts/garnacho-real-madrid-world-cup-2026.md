Alejandro Garnacho arrived at the 2026 FIFA World Cup with plenty to prove, and the 22-year-old Argentine winger has emphatically delivered. Four goals and three assists across the group stage have seen him emerge as one of the tournament's most electrifying attackers — and the watching world has taken notice.

Real Madrid, according to multiple sources close to the club, have had scouts permanently stationed in the stands monitoring Garnacho's every move. Los Blancos have long coveted the Manchester United wide man, and his performances on North American soil appear to have accelerated their interest into something approaching a concrete pursuit.

Garnacho's finest moment came in Argentina's 3-1 dismantling of Poland in their final group fixture in Dallas, where the forward cut in from the left, dummied two defenders, and curled an unstoppable effort into the top corner that drew breathless comparisons to his boyhood idol Cristiano Ronaldo. The clip has since amassed over 80 million views across social media.

Paris Saint-Germain and Bayern Munich are also said to have registered interest, with PSG particularly keen to bolster their attacking options following a turbulent domestic campaign. However, sources suggest Real Madrid remain the frontrunners, with club president Florentino Perez personally overseeing the operation.

Manchester United, who acquired Garnacho from Atletico Madrid's academy in 2020, are under no obligation to sell but are acutely aware that the player's contract expires in the summer of 2027. A fee in the region of £120 million has been mooted, though United are understood to be holding out for closer to £150 million.

For Argentina, Garnacho's form represents a generational passing of the torch. With Messi having stepped back, the Buenos Aires-born forward is increasingly inheriting that creative burden — and so far, he is wearing it magnificently.
