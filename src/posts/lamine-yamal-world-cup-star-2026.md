There are players who arrive at a World Cup with a reputation. And then there are players who arrive and build one in real time, before the watching world's eyes, in a matter of days.

Lamine Yamal is emphatically the latter — and at 18 years old, he is doing things at the 2026 FIFA World Cup that most players never manage in an entire career.

Five goal contributions across the group stage. An average of 4.3 successful dribbles per game — the highest of any outfield player in the tournament. A pass completion rate of 91 percent in the final third that defies his age, his position, and the pressure of the occasion. And throughout it all, a demeanour of absolute calm that borders on the unnerving.

Born in Esplugues de Llobregat to a Moroccan father and an Equatorial Guinean mother, Yamal joined Barcelona's La Masia academy at the age of seven. He made his first-team debut at 15 years and 290 days — the youngest player in La Liga history — and has not looked back since. Last season at the Camp Nou he posted 23 goals and 19 assists across all competitions. He is, by any reasonable measure, already one of the three best players on the planet.

At the World Cup, those qualities have been magnified. His acceleration is genuinely frightening — timed in training at 36.4km/h, making him faster than any defender who has tried to contain him. His left foot delivers the ball with a precision that makes the difficult look routine.

Comparisons to Messi have followed Yamal since he was a child. At this World Cup, for the first time, those comparisons feel entirely earned.
