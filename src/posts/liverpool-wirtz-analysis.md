The British transfer record has been broken before. What is different about Liverpool's acquisition of Florian Wirtz is not the number — though £117m will take some digesting — it is the profile. This is not a club buying a proven Premier League performer, nor a speculative punt on a young talent with potential. This is the best player on the continent at 22, arriving in England at the peak of his development curve.

Wirtz had 18 goals and 22 assists in Bundesliga football last season. More tellingly, he had six goals and nine assists in the Champions League — a competition that had, for two years, been cited as the environment where his game needed testing at the highest level. He passed that test comprehensively.

What he brings to Liverpool specifically is a creativity in the final third that the club has lacked since they stopped being able to rely on the synchronisation of Salah, Mané, and Firmino. Arne Slot has built a system that is hard to beat and easy to watch, but the side has occasionally lacked a player capable of finding solutions when the structured approach breaks down. Wirtz is that player.

The rest of the Premier League will feel this. City have spent the summer managing their financial situation carefully. Arsenal are building around younger talent. Chelsea are, as always, conducting several experiments at once. None of them have added a player of this quality at this stage of their career.

Liverpool are not just title contenders. They are, on paper, the team to beat in Europe next season too.
