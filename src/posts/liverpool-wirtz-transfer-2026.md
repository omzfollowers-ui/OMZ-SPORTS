Liverpool Football Club have completed the signing of German international Florian Wirtz from Bayer Leverkusen in a deal worth £118 million, making the 23-year-old the most expensive player in the club's history.

The transfer brings to an end a pursuit that stretched across three transfer windows. Liverpool head coach Arne Slot had identified Wirtz as his primary target for over a year, and the Merseyside club have now landed their man ahead of fierce competition from Manchester City and Real Madrid, both of whom submitted rival bids in recent weeks.

Wirtz arrives at Anfield having spent the last two seasons as arguably the finest playmaker in the Bundesliga — racking up 22 goals and 18 assists last season alone as Leverkusen claimed a third consecutive Bundesliga title. His ability to play as a classic number 10, drift wide, or operate as a second striker gives Slot a genuinely multi-dimensional weapon.

"Florian is one of the most complete midfielders in world football," said Slot at Sunday's press conference. "He sees the game differently to most players. We are absolutely delighted to bring him to Liverpool."

Wirtz, speaking for the first time as a Liverpool player, described the move as the fulfilment of a lifelong dream. "Liverpool is one of the biggest clubs in the world. When they came for me, there was only one answer. I cannot wait to play at Anfield."

The signing is widely expected to be the first of several major arrivals at Anfield this summer, with a centre-back and a striker also believed to be on the club's shortlist as they prepare for the new Premier League season.
