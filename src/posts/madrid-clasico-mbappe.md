The Santiago Bernabéu has seen plenty of statement performances, but few arrived with the cold efficiency of Saturday night. Kylian Mbappé needed just 27 first-half minutes to complete a hat-trick, turning a tense top-of-the-table fixture into a procession and dragging his side back to the summit with three games to play.

The opener came against the run of play — a loose touch in midfield, a forty-yard sprint, and a finish dispatched before the goalkeeper had set his feet. By the time the third went in, a near-post header from a corner he had no business reaching first, the visiting bench had stopped protesting and started planning for next season.

"He decides games on his own, and there is very little you can do about it," the opposing manager admitted afterward, refusing to single out his defenders. "We had a plan for twenty minutes. He had a plan for the whole night."

What makes the run more remarkable is the context. Three weeks ago this team sat fourth, eight points adrift and leaking goals. A change of shape, a settled back line, and a forward in the form of his life have rewritten the table. The remaining fixtures are kind. For the first time since August, the trophy is in their hands rather than someone else's.
