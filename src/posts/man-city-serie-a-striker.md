Manchester City have opened preliminary talks with Napoli over striker Lorenzo Ferrante, according to sources familiar with the negotiations. The 23-year-old scored 27 goals in Serie A last season and has emerged as the club's first-choice option as they plan for a post-Haaland era.

Haaland's contract runs until 2027 and the Norwegian has not yet signalled an intention to leave, but City's board is understood to be unwilling to enter the final year of a player of his value without a concrete succession plan in place. Ferrante represents that plan.

Napoli's position is firm at present — they want €75m and will not negotiate below that figure until the summer window officially opens. City's opening approach was exploratory rather than a formal bid, designed to establish whether a deal is structurally possible before resources are committed.

Ferrante has 14 caps for Italy and is considered a certainty for the Azzurri's next major tournament cycle. His profile — strong in the air, mobile enough to press, capable of creating as well as finishing — fits the system Pep Guardiola has been running since Haaland arrived.

Whether a deal materialises before the window closes in August remains unclear. City are also tracking a second option in the Bundesliga, though that target is understood to prefer remaining in Germany for at least one more season.
