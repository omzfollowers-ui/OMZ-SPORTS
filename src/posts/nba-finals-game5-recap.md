Oklahoma City's season should have ended in the third quarter of Game 5. Boston led by 18, were shooting 54 percent from deep, and Jayson Tatum had already matched his season-high points total before the break. What followed was one of the most complete halves of playoff basketball in recent Finals history.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander finished with 44 points, 9 assists, and a performance in the fourth quarter that had everyone in the arena aware they were watching something exceptional. He scored 19 of his points in the final 11 minutes, including back-to-back threes that swung the game irreversibly in the Thunder's favour at a moment when Boston appeared to have steadied.

The collapse will sting for the Celtics, and it will prompt searching questions about their bench depth. When their starting unit was rested briefly in the third quarter, OKC scored 14 unanswered points — a sequence that gave the Thunder both the lead and the belief that the gap between the teams was smaller than the first half suggested.

Game 6 is in Boston on Friday night. The Celtics have won 19 straight postseason games at TD Garden over three years. OKC have not won a championship since relocating from Seattle.

Chet Holmgren's status for Game 6 is uncertain after leaving late in the fourth quarter with a right ankle issue. He was seen walking without assistance post-game, but the Thunder medical staff will assess him overnight.
