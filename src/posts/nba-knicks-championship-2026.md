NEW YORK — The confetti has long since fallen on Madison Square Garden, the ticker-tape parade through the Canyon of Heroes is a memory etched into New York City's soul, and the champagne is spent. But for the 2026 NBA Champion New York Knicks, the work is far from over.

New York completed one of the most emotionally charged title runs in recent memory, dismantling the San Antonio Spurs 4-1 in the NBA Finals to claim the franchise's first championship since 1973 — a 53-year drought that had come to define an entire city's sporting psyche. Jalen Brunson, the team's heartbeat and undisputed alpha, silenced every last doubter with a postseason performance for the ages. Karl-Anthony Towns was a force of nature down low. The Knicks finished a staggering 16-3 in the playoffs — a run of dominance that few saw coming when the season began.

Now comes the harder part: defending it.

The blockbuster development everyone is still processing — Giannis Antetokounmpo has been traded from Milwaukee to the Miami Heat, a move that immediately reshapes the Eastern Conference around New York's throne. The Greek Freak reportedly pushed to join the Knicks, but New York and Milwaukee never got close to a deal.

Elsewhere, the Los Angeles Lakers secured a four-year extension for Austin Reaves as they continue building around Luka Doncic, while LeBron James, at 41 and an unrestricted free agent, is widely expected to return to LA on a veteran-minimum arrangement.

For the Knicks, the priority is clear — lock in the core, protect the dynasty before it begins, and don't blink. Broadway just had its best night in half a century.
