The summer window has been open for under three weeks and the volume of business already transacted is running ahead of last year's pace. Below is where the major stories stand as of Monday morning.

**Done and dusted**

Florian Wirtz completed his move to Liverpool for a British record fee of £117m on a five-year contract. The unveiling drew 40,000 fans to Anfield on a Tuesday afternoon. The German had been linked with every major European club for two years; Liverpool's project and their ability to offer Champions League football from day one proved decisive.

Kylian Mbappé's contract extension with Real Madrid is confirmed. There had been six weeks of speculation about a clause allowing him to leave this summer; that clause was removed as part of the new deal, which runs to 2030.

Aston Villa have signed Porto centre-back Martim Costa for £38m — a signing that suggests they are serious about addressing the defensive fragility that cost them points in the second half of last season.

**Advanced talks**

Arsenal's pursuit of Sporting CP midfielder Tomás Ferreira is at final-stage. A medical is expected next week. Fee agreed at €62m initial, with add-ons to €70m.

Chelsea are in discussions with Atlético Madrid over a 20-year-old forward who has been extraordinary in the Spanish youth system and is now attracting senior interest from multiple clubs.

**Collapsed**

Manchester United's attempt to re-sign a former academy graduate from Juventus broke down over image rights. The player has since agreed terms with Bayern Munich.

The window runs until the end of August. We will update this ledger as deals land.
