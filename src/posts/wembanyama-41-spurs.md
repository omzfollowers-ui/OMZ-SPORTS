There are individual performances, and then there are nights that bend a game around one player. Victor Wembanyama produced the latter on Thursday, finishing with 41 points, 16 rebounds, and seven blocks as San Antonio walked into the defending champions' building and walked out with a win few had predicted.

The blocks told the story as much as the points. Time and again a driving guard committed to a layup only to find the rim guarded by a seven-foot-four problem with a reach that makes the paint feel half its size. The champions shot under 38 percent inside the arc, a number that would embarrass them in a film session.

Offensively, the variety was the thing. Face-up jumpers, a turnaround from the post, one absurd stepback three over a smaller defender who did everything right and still got nowhere. He is no longer a prospect with upside. He is the gravitational centre of every possession he touches.

San Antonio are not a finished team, and the standings still place them outside the picture that matters in spring. But on nights like this, the gap between where they are and where they are going looks a great deal smaller.
