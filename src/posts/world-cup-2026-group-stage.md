The group stage of the 48-team World Cup closed with the kind of chaos that comes from too many permutations and not enough time to keep up. By the final whistle on the last match of each group, the picture was clearer — though not always prettier.

Brazil topped Group E without breaking sweat, with Endrick delivering on the promise that had clubs across Europe offering fees north of €90m eighteen months ago. Their defence gave up one goal across three games. Whoever faces them next will need a plan that few have shown they possess.

England's progression was comfortable on paper and nervous in practice. Three wins, six goals scored, three conceded — but the manner of the Portugal match, where they were outpossessed for 65 minutes before a late winner, will have given the coaching staff more to work on than the scoreline suggests.

Germany's early exit will be studied for years. A side rebuilt at considerable cost, eliminated by a Japan side playing with a clarity of system that the Germans simply could not disrupt. Head coach Thomas Tuchel faces the harshest post-tournament review of his career.

The standout performer of the group stage, by most measures, was Lamine Yamal. Spain have an abundance of talent but the 18-year-old was their sharpest edge — two goals, four assists, and a running threat that no full-back in the group phase managed to contain for a full 90 minutes. The knockouts begin Thursday.
