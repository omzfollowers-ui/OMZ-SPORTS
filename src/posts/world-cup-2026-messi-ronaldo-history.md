NORTH AMERICA — The 2026 FIFA World Cup knockout stage is officially underway, and it has opened with a chapter straight from the history books. On a remarkable Sunday, the tournament's two greatest names — Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo — wrote themselves into the record books for perhaps the final time on the world's grandest stage.

In Dallas, at AT&T Stadium, Messi delivered the kind of moment that defines careers — except his career has been made entirely of such moments. The Argentine legend scored in Argentina's final Group J match against Jordan, becoming the first player in World Cup history to score against 13 different national teams. His 19th World Cup goal stretches his all-time tournament record further into the stratosphere. With first place already secured, Messi came on in his own time — and still produced.

Meanwhile, Cristiano Ronaldo made his 25th World Cup appearance, drawing level with Lothar Matthäus for second on the all-time appearances list. Only Messi stands ahead of him. At 41 years old and appearing in his sixth World Cup, Ronaldo walked past reporters without stopping — his expression telling a story of a competitor who felt he had more to give. His two goals from the group stage, both in Portugal's 5-0 demolition of Uzbekistan, confirmed he is still dangerous when the service arrives.

Today, the knockouts begin in earnest. Canada face South Africa in Los Angeles in one of the day's headline fixtures, with a boisterous home crowd expected to roar the hosts into the last 16 for the first time in their history.

The bracket is set, the giants are alive, and the 48-team World Cup is about to become something truly extraordinary. The greatest show on Earth is only just warming up.
