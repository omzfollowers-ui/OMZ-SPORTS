If the 2026 FIFA World Cup group stage has produced one undeniable truth, it is this: Spain are frighteningly good, and nobody has yet found an answer to them.

Three games, three wins, 11 goals scored, one conceded. The numbers are startling — but it is the manner of Spain's victories that has sent scouts and coaches from rival nations scrambling to their tactical boards. This is not the patient, possession-for-possession's-sake Spain of the 2010 era. This is something faster, more vertical, and arguably more dangerous.

Under head coach Luis de la Fuente, Spain have evolved. The spine remains familiar — goalkeeper David Raya commanding in his box, Rodri conducting the midfield tempo with metronomic authority — but the attacking third has been transformed. Lamine Yamal, at just 18, has been the tournament's single most exciting player. His interplay with fellow speedster Nico Williams on the opposite flank has created a corridor of chaos that no full-back pairing has successfully managed.

In their group stage finale against Morocco, Spain moved the ball through 40 consecutive passes before Williams simply accelerated past two defenders as though they were standing still and cut back for Yamal to finish. It lasted 12 seconds. It was effortless.

"We have not shown everything yet," De la Fuente said coolly after the final whistle. It was the most alarming sentence spoken by any manager at this tournament.

Spain face the United States in the Round of 32 on July 2nd. The Americans will be buoyed by their home support — but on current form, La Roja look unstoppable.
